about us

Robinsong specializes in natural resource management, from ecological landscaping, urban wild bird sanctuaries, corporate wildlife habitats, land-use planning for conservation and Wetland and Stream restoration on a large scale.

Morgan County

In 1998 Robinsong opened Alabamaâ€™s first Wetland Mitigation Bank in Morgan County, a 650 acre cattle farm site that we fully restored to its historic wetland function. We plugged the 30 ditches that were put in to drain the site and created ponds to hold the water longer. We planted 200,000+ native hardwood trees and monitored the site annually for 7 years to record the successful restoration process. We donated 4 acres of high ground along Highway 36 near Hartselle, AL to Morgan County and they developed the site for the USDA Rural Development Center, the NRCS Conservation Center and the Flint Creek Conservation District which now manages the conservation easement that protects this restored wetland for perpetuity.

Flint River Mitigation Bank

This site is 700 acres bordered by Highway 72E on the north and sits in the triangle created by the Flint River and Hurricane Creek. Thus far in Phase One we have restored 133 acres of wetlands and 1.5 miles of stream, a tributary of Hurricane Creek, planting 70,000+ native trees. We have begun the baseline documentation for Phase Two where we propose restoring 240 acres of wetland and one mile of stream feeding the Flint River.
